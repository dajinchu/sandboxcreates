Special Needs Calculator

Some students, particularly ones with Autism, can have difficulty seeing the decimal point on a normal calculator. Therefore, Mrs. Wrenn, a special education teacher at Worcester Public Schools contracted us to build a calculator that would make the decimal point more visible. Together, we decided on the decimal point being larger and a different color (red). With this goal in mind, the Sandbox team designed, engineered, and coded an Arduino based calculator. Currently, the calculator is being tested with students at Worcester Public Schools with Mrs. Wrenn, who reports that one of her students “absolutely LOVES the new calculator.”

Sandbox Gesture Calculator

How often do you use your calculator on your smartphone to calculate something more complicated than a tip? Probably never. Why? Well, calculator apps are either too simple to do anything more sophisticated, or too cluttered to be useful. This is where Sandbox Calculator comes in. Re-inventing how we interact with numbers on calculators, Sandbox fully takes advantage of the touch-screen to offer an unique gesture based experience. This award winning app (Congressional App Challenge) has received rave reviews with Mr. Konate from Harvard University saying, “this should be the default calculator on every phone.” To learn more about this calculator, visit sandboxcalc.com.
